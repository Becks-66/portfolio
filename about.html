The path that brought me here hasn't always been linear, but it has been varied, constantly pushing me out of my comfort zone and forcing me to learn.

I have always been fascinated by art and design, finding every possible way to vent my curiosity, whether by drawing, crafting objects, or writing new worlds into existence.

In high school, I studied jewelry design and manufacturing in Florence, a path that I loved and that taught me the fundamentals of craftsmanship. At the same time, I developed a strong interest in how human beings think and feel. Like a magnet, I was drawn to psychology. That universe captivated me for five years, leading to my Master's degree. I focused on the applied side of the field: Human-Computer Interaction, economic decision-making, and User Experience.

In 2025, I completed a six-month research internship at the National Research Council (CNR) in Pisa (Tuscany), studying trust and reliance in user-AI interactions, a topic I became deeply passionate about.

Throughout my studies, I never stopped creating, drawing, and experimenting with new media.

To look at some of my works, you can check my instagram (@reb_fa_cose)!
