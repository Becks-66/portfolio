Busuu

Redesign of an app for learning language.

Type of project: UX Project
Process: Redesign

Reading time: 6 minutes

User story

Elisa, 23, plans to move abroad to Germany with her sister to start a new life and share living costs. However, they don't know German!

They have a year of time to learn the language while they are busy with university, so they decide to download Busuu, a mobile app for flexible language learning. After two months of use, they've encountered some difficulties.

How can we help them improve their learning experience with the app?

About Busuu

Busuu is a mobile app for language learning. As other apps of this kind, it is designed for busy adult, who are already occupied with work and study. It offers courses in 13 different languages (e.g. english, spanish, french, japanese, german, chinese, russian).

Each course is divided into short chapters, further broken down into 1–3 minute steps, making it easy to complete lessons during a lunch break or before bedtime.

Problems

All observations are based on the A1 German course, but the issues likely apply to other courses as well.

The course lacks a structured grammar learning plan. Although there is a dedicated grammar review section, its exercises rely on vocabulary from lessons not yet covered, making it difficult to use effectively for learners who want to study grammar independently.

The app does not adequately support learning approaches beyond its linear main course, failing to account for users' diverse learning strategies.

Vocabulary and grammar exercises lack variety and do not encourage flexible word usage.

The review section's UI is cluttered and unclear: labels like "weak" and "strong" are ambiguous and uninformative, which adds to the confusion.

Research questions

How can we make the learning experience with the app more structured and flexible?

How can we make the learning experience more fun?

Redesign process

Rethink the Review section:

  • Introduce new grammar exercises tailored for users learning at their own pace, separate from the main course.
  • Simplify the UI by replacing vague labels like "weak" with a clear status bar showing progress in reviewed grammar topics.

Add a Stories section: users can learn the language by reading or listening to simple stories organized by difficulty levels (e.g., A1, A2, B1). After each story, users can take a quiz to test their comprehension.

Add a Battle section: enable competitions with mini-games and quizzes among friends in the app community. Battles occur weekly, and users will have 24-hour to complete them. Leaderboards update to show scores among friends and the entire Busuu community. Users earn badges based on performance and consistency, tracking progress over time.

Testing

Since Busuu is a language learning app, testing should be conducted over an extended period to evaluate improvements in user experience and learning outcomes. Users should use the updated app version for two weeks and complete questionnaires to provide feedback.

Experimental design

  • Participants: 20 participants, aged 20 to 55 (the age range should be decided based on the data concerning the age of the users).
  • Testing duration: 2 weeks.
  • Instructions: participants will use the app as much as possible during the testing period and complete two short questionnaires at the end of each week. They should aim to use the app at least six days a week for a minimum of 10 minutes per session. If they miss a session, they should report it in the questionnaires along with the reason. Additionally, users are encouraged to explore as many sections of the app as they can.

Measures

Frequency of use: how often participants used the app during the week.

In the questionnaire:

  • How many times did you use the app during the week?
    (0 - 1 - 2 - 3- 4 - 5 - 6)
  • If you missed one or more sessions, select one or more of the following reasons:
    (I was busy / I wasn't interested in using the app / I was bored by the app / I forgot using the app)

Daily time spent on the app: interpreted as a measure of user engagement.

In the questionnaire:

  • How much did each session approximately last?
    (0-10 minutes / 10-20 minutes / 20-30 minutes / 30 - 40 minutes / more than 40 minutes)

Learning flexibility: how flexible and varied users found the app's learning strategies.

In the questionnaire:

  • Did you use other section beside the main course one?
    (Yes / No)
  • If yes, indicate which ones.
  • How varied did you find the learning strategy proposed by the app?
    (5 point Likert scale)
  • How flexible did you find the learning strategy proposed by the app?
    (5 point Likert scale)
  • Did the flexible learning options match your preferred learning style and pace?
    (Yes/No + open feedback)

Engagement with Battle feature: how fun and motivating participants found the Battle section.

In the questionnaire:

  • How motivating do you find the new "battle" section and community competitions?
    (Likert scale + "I haven't tried it)
  • How fun do you find the new "battle" section and community competitions?
    (5 point Likert scale / "I haven't tried it")

Enjoyment and usefulness of Stories section.

In the questionnaire:

  • Did the "stories" section help you learn vocabulary and grammar more effectively?
    (Yes/No + "I haven't tried it")
  • How enjoyable did you find the "stories" section?
    (5 point Likert scale + "I haven't tried it")

Overall perceived usefulness and enjoyment of the app.

In the questionnaire:

  • Overall, how fun did you find using Busuu?
    (5 point Likert scale)
  • Overall, how useful did you find Busuu?
    (5 point Likert scale)
  • Will you use the app again after the testing? If no, why?
    (Yes/No)
  • Will you suggest the app to one your friends, relative or collegues? If no, why?
    (Yes/No)
  • What improvements, if any, would you suggest for the explored features?
    (Open feedback)

Expected outcomes

  • An high frequency of use
  • An high amount of daily time spent on the app.
  • An high score in the evaluation of learning flexibility.
  • An high score in the perceived usefulness and enjoyment of the app.
